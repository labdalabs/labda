# Labda

### The research-acceleration platform for bioscience

We pay scientists fairly for the expertise AI currently scrapes for free —
and turn it into tools that push biology forward faster.

*Pre-seed · Poland · 2026*

---

## The problem

- **AI labs are starving for expert data.** Frontier models have run out of internet; the next gains need real scientific reasoning, verified results, and RL environments — from actual PhDs.
- **Scientists are broke and underserved.** A bioscience PhD earns ~€1,000–1,500/mo and works with clunky, decade-old software.
- **Science itself is slow and unreproducible.** Manual data labeling, interpretation, and a well-documented reproducibility crisis bottleneck discovery.

> Three problems, one missing piece: **no one connects the scientists to the demand — or to better tools.**

---

## The solution

A platform that does three things in sequence, each funding the next:

1. **A free AI copilot** for computational biologists → builds a loyal community.
2. **"Research-as-an-API"** → AI labs and companies commission verified expert work; the data is clean, consented exhaust we sell.
3. **Specialized ML tools** (cell/genome segmentation, spatial auto-labeling) → built from watching real workflows, they elevate scientists instead of replacing them.

**Come for the tool. Stay for the community. We monetize the demand, never the scientist.**

---

## How it works — the flywheel

```
Free copilot  ──►  Engaged comp-bio community
      ▲                       │
      │                       ▼
 Better tools  ◄──  Research-as-API marketplace (MCP)
      ▲                       │
      │                       ▼
  Field ML  ◄──  Verified, provenance-tagged data  ──►  AI labs ($)
```

- Each commissioned task = **paid work for a scientist + verified training data for a buyer.**
- More tasks → more data → ML that makes scientists faster → more reasons to stay.
- **The sequencing is the moat.**

---

## The product

**L0 — Copilot (live first):** literature + experiment/analysis assistant for bioinformatics. The daily-use wedge.

**L1 — Marketplace / MCP:** an *async* "submit research task → verified result with provenance" API. Two front doors: MCP for AI agents, dashboard for companies. *Done right, not stupidly fast — the anti-Mechanical-Turk.*

**L1.x — Verification network:** on-demand, cross-lab replication of findings. Attacks the reproducibility crisis; produces the rarest data of all.

**L3 — Field ML:** vision/segmentation tools per subfield. The mission payoff.

---

## Why now

- **Expert data is the hottest spend in AI** — labs are paying record sums for verified human reasoning, evals, and RL environments.
- **MCP makes "human research as a tool call" newly possible** — agents can dispatch real expert work programmatically.
- **Foundation models can't do the hard science** — but they *can* be made to label, draft, and accelerate, which creates demand for human-verified ground truth.
- **A generation of AI-native, underpaid scientists** is ready to participate.

---

## Who pays

| Buyer | Buys | When |
|---|---|---|
| **AI labs** (first) | Commissioned research + data license | Stream A — first revenue |
| **Biotechs / companies** | Research-via-API for their own use | Stream B |
| **Journals / researchers** | Cross-lab verification of findings | Stream B |

Scientists never pay. The free tool stays free forever — they are the supply, the community, and the source of value.

---

## Business model

**Two revenue streams per task:**

1. **Service take rate** — ~20–30% on fulfillment.
2. **Data license** — AI labs pay *separately* for rights to the verified output. High-margin; long-term the profit engine.

Fulfillment can run near break-even while the **data license carries the margin**. Quality tiers (single / consensus-verified / senior-reviewed) are the pricing lever — exactly how labs already buy data.

---

## Moat — why not Mercor/Surge/Scale?

The marketplace is commoditizing. **We don't compete as a marketplace.**

1. **We own the daily workflow** via a free tool + community. Brokers rent labor and must re-acquire it constantly; our scientists are loyal to a product they use every day.
2. **Vertical depth** in comp-bio beats horizontal breadth.
3. **Data → automation flywheel** drives our marginal cost below any pure broker's.
4. **Augment-not-replace + contributor revenue-share** — scientists share in the tools they train. No labor broker can credibly copy that.

---

## Competition

| Slice | Who | What they lack |
|---|---|---|
| Expert-data marketplaces | Mercor, Surge, Scale | No tool, no community, no vertical depth |
| AI research tools | Elicit, Consensus | No marketplace, no data product |
| Lab notebooks | Benchling, protocols.io | Industry-only / not AI-native; no earnings loop |
| Verification | OSF, ResearchHub | Not a paid, on-demand, data-generating service |

**Everyone owns a slice. No one owns the loop.**

---

## Go-to-market

- **Land narrow:** computational biology / bioinformatics — digital-native, immediately fulfillable, AI-lab-relevant.
- **Bottom-up, community-led:** seed where scientists already gather; never sell to the university.
- **Transparency as a movement:** "fair pay for the expertise AI scrapes for free."
- **Concierge first:** prove the loop by hand and self-fund a trickle of paid tasks → a curated **sample data batch** that closes the first AI-lab design partners. *Build the tool for real; fake the marketplace until proven.*
