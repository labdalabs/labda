# Labda
### The operating system for experimental science.

> **Investor / accelerator one-pager.** External-facing narrative (contrast: the rest of `docs/strategy/` is blunt internal working docs).
> **Last updated:** 2026-06-17

**Thesis.** The lab is one of the last serious workplaces still run on disconnected, manual tools. Unifying it is both a better way to do science *and* the foundation for the verified data that AI in biology will need.

**1 · The problem**
Science runs on tools that don't talk. A single experiment spans microscope software, spreadsheets, plotting apps, PDFs, and notebooks. The work is redone by hand every time data moves, nothing is reusable, nothing is shared, and the thinking drowns in repetitive busywork.

**2 · The product**
Labda puts the whole experiment on one canvas. Lab machines, data, analysis, and literature — all connected and wired into workflows that rerun with one click.

An AI coworker rides along. It won't just agree. It flags weak spots and challenges the hypothesis and protocol before the reviewers do.

It runs as a plugin in the editor researchers already use. Nothing to migrate.

**3 · The wedge**
Win where the pain is sharpest and most repeated: the grind of turning raw instrument output into results. We automate that loop first for one kind of bench scientist — collapsing a day of manual counting and re-plotting into a click — and earn their trust before expanding outward across fields.

**4 · The moat — a data flywheel**
The tool is open and free; that earns the community. On top sits a *vetted* network — GitHub for the lab — where members share and build on each other's workflows, models, and protocols instead of locking them in a drawer.

Then it compounds. Every experiment run in Labda produces structured, verified, consented process-data — the reasoning *and* the result, not just the final paper. That data trains the automations that make the tool better, which pulls in more researchers, who produce more data. Each turn sharpens quality and widens the lead — a flywheel no point tool or closed incumbent can spin.

That loop is also the business: premium features and a marketplace for commissioned work and cross-lab verification today; over time, the largest store of verified experimental process-data in existence — exactly what the next generation of biology AI is starved for.

**Why now.** AI finally makes the antagonistic copilot real, and instruments are converging on open formats — the lab is integrable for the first time.
